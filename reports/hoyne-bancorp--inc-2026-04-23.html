Verdict: Hoyne Bancorp is a well-capitalized micro-thrift with strong asset quality and an improving NIM, but near-breakeven profitability, a heavy CD funding mix, and a stretched efficiency ratio make the current ~1.57x TBV valuation difficult to justify. The bank is executing a strategic pivot toward commercial lending that has potential, but is not yet earning its cost of capital. Investors should wait for evidence of sustained profitability before committing capital at current prices.
Hoyne Bancorp completed its mutual-to-stock conversion in December 2024, raising ~$79M into a $455M asset base — resulting in a massively overcapitalized balance sheet with a Tier 1 leverage ratio of 20%, roughly double what regulators require. This capital surplus is both a blessing and a curse: it protects against credit losses but severely dilutes returns on equity, and the bank posted near-zero net income on a nine-month basis in 2025. The efficiency ratio in the 85-90% range is a major concern, meaning the bank spends roughly $0.87-0.90 for every dollar of revenue earned — well above the 55% threshold for a well-run community bank. The NIM expansion from 2.36% to 3.27% year-over-year is genuinely encouraging and suggests the commercial loan pivot is beginning to bear fruit, but the deposit structure remains heavily CD-dependent, which will keep funding costs elevated and compress the NIM tailwind. Asset quality is a genuine bright spot — NPAs at 0.4% of assets and ACL coverage of 327.7% of NPLs reflect prudent underwriting — but at 1.57x TBV with ROE well below 5%, the stock has priced in a turnaround that has not yet materialized in the financials.
Criterion Metric Notes
✓ 1. Assets Under $50B $454.8M total assets Hoyne is a micro-community bank at under $500M in assets, well within the regional/thrift profile. No systemic risk or regulatory complexity concerns at this size.
✗ 2. Efficiency Ratio ~85-90% estimated The efficiency ratio is severely elevated, reflecting a cost structure that far exceeds revenue generation. Near-breakeven net income on $9.9M in net interest income confirms the bank is not yet operating efficiently; target is under 55%.
⚠ 3. Loan-to-Deposit Ratio ~70% ($249M loans / $357M deposits) The LTD ratio of 70% is below the 80-90% sweet spot, indicating the bank is underdeploying its deposit base. This is partly a post-IPO phenomenon given the large capital infusion, but it suppresses earning asset yields and NIM potential.
⚠ 4. Net Interest Margin 3.27% Q3 2025 (up from 2.36% Q3 2024) NIM has expanded meaningfully and crossed the 3% threshold, which is a positive trend. However, the heavy CD funding mix poses a risk to sustaining this improvement as CDs reprice, and the trajectory needs several more quarters of confirmation.
✓ 5. CRE Concentration Commercial loans ~50% of ~$252M gross loan book; equity ~$90M With total equity of $90M and a commercial loan book estimated at roughly $125M, CRE concentration appears well below the 300% of capital regulatory threshold. The strategic pivot to commercial is still early-stage.
✓ 6. Non-Performing Loans NPAs 0.4% of total assets; ACL 0.94% of loans; ACL/NPL coverage 327.7% Asset quality is excellent. NPAs are well below the 1% caution threshold, and ACL coverage of NPLs at 327.7% provides a substantial buffer. This is a genuine strength of the franchise.
✗ 7. Tangible Book Value ~1.57x TBV at $15.72 vs. ~$10.00 TBV/share at IPO At 1.57x TBV, the stock exceeds the 1.5x threshold that would require ROE above 15% to justify. With ROE well below 5% on near-breakeven earnings, the current valuation is not supported by fundamentals and reflects speculative thrift conversion premium.
✗ 8. Deposit Structure Heavy CD concentration; CD interest expense drives majority of $4.88M deposit costs The deposit mix is CD-heavy, which is the most expensive and least sticky form of funding. Limited disclosure on low-cost checking/savings balances is a concern; this structure will pressure NIM and limits the franchise's competitive moat.
⚠ 9. Management Quality Strategic pivot to commercial lending underway; 2024 net loss of $2.0M Management is executing a deliberate strategic shift from residential to commercial lending, which shows forward-thinking but also introduces execution risk. The 2024 net loss followed 2023 profitability, and near-breakeven 2025 results suggest the turnaround is incomplete. Insufficient qualitative disclosure to fully assess management commentary tone.
✓ 10. Capital Ratios Tier 1 leverage ratio: 20.0%; no FHLB borrowings Capital ratios are exceptional — a 20% Tier 1 leverage ratio is roughly 2.5x the well-capitalized threshold. Zero borrowings confirm the fortress balance sheet. While overcapitalization is a drag on ROE, it eliminates near-term capital risk entirely.
Green flags
✓ NIM expanded dramatically from 2.36% to 3.27% year-over-year — strongest positive signal in the filing
✓ Excellent asset quality: NPAs at 0.4% of assets and ACL coverage of NPLs at 327.7%
✓ Zero FHLB borrowings — entirely deposit-funded with no wholesale funding dependency
✓ Tier 1 leverage ratio of 20% provides extraordinary capital cushion against any credit cycle
✓ Strategic pivot to commercial lending (~50% of book) is the right long-term direction for margin improvement
✓ 137-year operating history (founded 1887) suggests deep community roots and stable core deposit relationships
✓ Allowance for credit losses at 0.94% of loans is prudently reserved relative to actual NPL experience
Red flags
✗ Efficiency ratio of 85-90% is far above peer norms and indicates the bank is barely covering its operating costs
✗ Near-breakeven net income (~$31K) for nine months ended September 30, 2025 despite $454M in assets
✗ Heavy reliance on CDs as primary funding source — expensive, rate-sensitive, and non-sticky deposits
✗ Stock trading at 1.57x TBV with ROE well below 5% — valuation not supported by current earnings power
✗ 2024 posted a net loss of $2.0M, raising questions about earnings consistency and trajectory
✗ Loan-to-deposit ratio of 70% signals significant underdeployment of the capital raised in the IPO
✗ Post-IPO overcapitalization (20% Tier 1) will suppress ROE for multiple years without aggressive capital deployment or buybacks
