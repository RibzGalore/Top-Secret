Verdict: Hoyne Bancorp is a small, well-capitalized thrift conversion carrying significant operational challenges — near-breakeven profitability, a heavy CD funding structure, and an elevated efficiency ratio undermine an otherwise clean credit profile. The NIM expansion is encouraging, but the bank must demonstrate it can translate that into meaningful earnings before the current market premium is justified. At 1.57x TBV with sub-1% ROA, the valuation is stretched.
Hoyne is a classic mutual thrift conversion story: a 138-year-old community institution that raised ~$79M in IPO proceeds and is now sitting on a fortress balance sheet with a 20% Tier 1 leverage ratio and zero borrowings — capital that far exceeds what the business currently needs. The NIM improvement from 2.36% to 3.27% year-over-year is the most compelling data point in this filing, suggesting the asset repricing cycle and commercial loan pivot are beginning to take hold. However, the efficiency ratio in the 85-90% range is alarming for a thrift of this size — virtually all net interest income is consumed by operating expenses, leaving almost nothing for shareholders. The strategic shift to 50% commercial loans introduces execution risk for an institution historically focused on residential lending, and the CD-heavy deposit mix means funding costs will remain elevated and sticky. Until the bank can demonstrate a sustainable path to a sub-70% efficiency ratio and meaningful ROE, the current stock price at 1.57x TBV prices in a successful transformation that has not yet materialized.

✗ 2. Efficiency Ratio ~85-90% (estimated) Non-interest expense of ~$9.5M against net interest income of ~$9.9M and ~$600K non-interest income leaves virtually no pre-tax income. This is far above the 55% target and signals the bank cannot currently cover its cost structure from operations.
⚠ 3. Loan-to-Deposit Ratio ~70% ($249M loans / $357M deposits) Below the 80-90% sweet spot, indicating the bank is underdeploying its deposit base. With $0 in borrowings and excess capital from the IPO, there is significant room to grow the loan book, but execution risk is elevated given the commercial pivot.
✓ 4. Net Interest Margin 3.27% Q3 2025 (up from 2.36% Q3 2024) NIM has expanded materially year-over-year and crossed the 3% threshold, which is a meaningful positive signal. The trajectory from 2.93% in Q1 to 3.27% in Q3 suggests continued improvement, though absolute dollar NII remains thin given the asset base.
